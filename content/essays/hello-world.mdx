This is the first thing I'm putting here, so: hi. I'm Arman. I build things for the web, mobile, and lately a lot of AI. The day job is mental health tech at a company called Somethings.

I've tried to start a blog maybe four times. Every time it goes the same way: I spend the whole weekend on the design, write one post, then never open it again. So I'm not going to pretend this time is different. I'll write when I actually have something, and the something is usually the same kind of thing: I built a small tool, or I broke something at work and figured out why, or I read about how some system holds together and want to get it straight in my own head. Mostly web, mobile, and the unglamorous parts of making AI features hold up for real people.

First post up is [git-tidy](/writing/git-tidy), a little CLI I made for deleting dead git branches without that low-grade fear that you just threw away something you needed. That's the kind of thing you'll get here. Specific, usually small, occasionally something I got wrong.

No schedule, no newsletter. I'm writing this down mostly so I remember what I figured out. If it helps you too, good. If something here is useful, or flat wrong, tell me. I'm not hard to find.
