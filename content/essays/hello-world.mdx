This is the first thing I'm putting here, so: hi. I'm Arman. I build things for the web, mobile, and lately a lot of AI. The day job is mental health tech at a company called Somethings.

I've started a blog and quit maybe four times. Same story each round: I spend the weekend fiddling with the design, write one post, then never come back. So I'm keeping the bar on the floor this time.

Here's roughly what I'll write about.

Small tools I build, and why I bothered. First one up is [git-tidy](/writing/git-tidy), a CLI for deleting dead git branches without that low-grade fear that you just nuked something you needed.

Things I learn the hard way at work. Mostly web and mobile, plus the unglamorous parts of making AI features actually hold up for real people.

And whatever I'm reading or poking at on the side. Right now that's system design and distributed systems.

No schedule, no newsletter. I'm mostly writing this down so I remember what I figured out, and if it happens to help you, even better. If something here is useful, or wrong, tell me. I'm not hard to find.
